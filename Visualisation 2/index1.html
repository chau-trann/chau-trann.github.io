Causes of Death Over Time

Evaluating the health of a population is most commonly measured by examining mortality, including metrics like child mortality and life expectancy, which are derived from mortality estimates. However, concentrating solely on mortality overlooks the broader impact of diseases, as they not only result in death but also inflict suffering on those living with them. A comprehensive assessment of health outcomes should consider both mortality and morbidity, encompassing prevalent diseases, to offer a more holistic perspective on the overall health status.
